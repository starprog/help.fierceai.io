### Prompt Engineering Tips

**Universal Best Practices:**
- Be specific about scene composition
- Describe desired motion and camera work
- Mention lighting and atmosphere
- Specify aesthetic or style
- Include mood and emotion descriptors

**Text-to-Video Prompts:**
```
[Subject] [Action] in [Environment], [Camera angle/movement], 
[Lighting], [Style/Mood], [Additional details]

Example:
A majestic lion walking through tall grass at sunset, 
slow motion wide shot tracking beside the lion, 
golden hour lighting with sun rays through grass, 
cinematic and epic mood, dust particles in air
```

**Image-to-Video Prompts:**
```
[Type of motion] [Speed] [Camera behavior] [Environmental effects]

Example:
Gentle swaying motion, slow and subtle, fixed camera position, 
soft wind rustling leaves, natural breathing and blinking
```

**Negative Prompt Tips:**
- List specific things to avoid
- Include quality descriptors to exclude
- Mention unwanted styles or effects

```
Example Negative Prompt:
blurry, low quality, distorted, unnatural motion, choppy animation, 
weird artifacts, uncanny valley, pixelated, grainy
```

### Camera Movement Control

**Available in:** Kling 2.5 Turbo Pro Text-to-Video

**Movement Types:**
- **None (Auto):** AI decides (recommended for most cases)
- **Horizontal:** Side-to-side panning (landscapes, following subjects)
- **Vertical:** Up-down movement (tall buildings, waterfalls)
- **Pan:** Smooth horizontal camera pan (cinematic reveals)
- **Tilt:** Up-down camera tilt (looking up at skyscrapers)
- **Zoom:** Zoom in/out (emphasizing subject, dramatic reveals)
- **Roll:** Rotating camera (creative/disorienting effects)

**Best Practices:**
- Start with Auto for most generations
- Use specific movements when narrative requires it
- Match movement to scene (slow pan for landscapes, zoom for reveals)
- Avoid overusing Roll (can be disorienting)

### CFG Scale (Kling 2.5 Pro)

**Range:** 0.0 to 1.0
**Default:** 0.5

**Scale Guide:**
- **0.0-0.3:** Very creative, loose interpretation, artistic freedom
- **0.4-0.6:** Balanced (recommended range)
- **0.7-1.0:** Strict prompt adherence, less creative variation

**When to Adjust:**
- **Lower CFG:** Artistic projects, when you want surprises
- **Higher CFG:** Technical videos, specific requirements, brand consistency

### Loop Video Feature

**Available in:** Kling 2.5 Turbo Pro (5-second mode only)

**Creates:** Seamlessly looping video that repeats smoothly

**Use Cases:**
- Website backgrounds
- Social media content
- GIF-style animations
- Ambient video displays
- Repeating motions (waves, fire, etc.)

**Tips:**
- Describe repeating or cyclical motion
- Avoid directional movement that can't loop naturally
- Test with subjects like: waves, fire, floating objects, ambient scenes

**Q: Can I generate longer than 12 seconds?**
A: Current maximum is 12 seconds (Sora). Generate multiple clips and stitch together for longer videos. Future updates may increase duration limits.

**Q: Why did my generation fail?**
A: Common reasons:
- Prompt violates content policy
- Reference image dimensions don't match output
- Insufficient credits
- Service temporarily unavailable
- Prompt too complex or ambiguous

Try:
- Simplify prompt
- Enable auto-fix (Veo models)
- Check reference image quality
- Rephrase to avoid policy issues

**Q: Should I disable audio to save credits?**
A: Disable audio when:
- Adding custom soundtrack later
- Creating silent background videos
- Budget is tight
- Video will be used without sound

Keep audio when:
- Natural ambient sound enhances content
- No post-processing planned
- Audio synchronization is important

- Credit attribution may be required

- Verify specific provider terms (Sora, Veo, etc.)

**Q: How long does generation take?**
A: Varies by provider and complexity:
