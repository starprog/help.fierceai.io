# Brand Voice

## Overview

**Brand Voice** is a powerful feature that allows you to create consistent, on-brand AI-generated content across all your projects. Instead of repeatedly introducing your company details in every AI interaction, Brand Voice stores your company information and automatically applies it to AI content generation, ensuring all output aligns with your brand identity.

Generate AI content exclusive to your brand and eliminate the need for repetitive introductions of your company with every request.

## Creating a Brand Voice

### Step 1: Company Information

Click **"New Company"** to create a new brand voice profile.

#### Required Fields

**Company Name** <span style="color: #ef4444;">*</span>
- The official name of your business entity
- Example: "FierceAI Technologies Inc."

**Industry**
- Select or enter multiple industries your company operates in
- Supports custom industry tags
- Examples: "Technology", "SaaS", "AI Services"

**Description** <span style="color: #ef4444;">*</span>
- A concise summary describing your company
- Include mission, vision, and what sets you apart
- Recommended: 2-3 paragraphs
- This description helps AI understand your company's context

**Website**
- Full URL of your company's official website
- Format: `https://www.yourcompany.com`

**Tagline**
- A memorable phrase encapsulating your mission or value proposition
- Keep it concise and impactful
- Example: "AI-Powered Content Creation Made Simple"

**Tone of Voice**
- Select the default writing style for your brand
- Options include:
  - Professional
  - Funny
  - Casual
  - Excited
  - Witty
  - Sarcastic
  - Feminine
  - Masculine
  - Bold
  - Dramatic
  - Grumpy
  - Secretive

**Target Audience**
- Describe the primary demographic your company targets
- Include age range, interests, professional level, etc.
- Example: "B2B decision makers, marketing managers, content creators aged 25-45"

**Brand Logo**
- Upload your company logo (optional)
- Recommended formats: PNG, JPG, SVG
- Helps with visual brand recognition

**Brand Color**
- Pick your primary brand color in HEX format
- Default: `#8fd2d0`
- Used for icon container shapes and visual elements
- Example: `#667eea`

**Specific Instructions**
- Define custom writing rules and preferences
- Examples:
  - Tone requirements: "Always maintain a friendly but professional tone"
  - Punctuation rules: "Use Oxford commas"
  - Banned words: "Never use jargon like 'synergy' or 'leverage'"
  - Sentence structure: "Keep sentences under 20 words when possible"
  - Style preferences: "Use active voice, avoid passive constructions"
  - Formatting: "Use bullet points for lists of 3+ items"

## Best Practices

### Creating Effective Brand Voices

**1. Be Detailed in Descriptions**
- Provide comprehensive company descriptions
- Include unique selling propositions
- Mention company culture and values
- Add competitive positioning

**2. Define Clear Guidelines**
- Specify exact tone requirements
- List banned words or phrases
- Define sentence structure preferences
- Include formatting rules

**3. Keep Information Updated**
- Review brand voice settings quarterly
- Update product descriptions when launching new offerings
- Adjust tone as your brand evolves
- Remove discontinued products

**4. Use Specific Examples**
```
Good: "Use short, punchy sentences. Maximum 15 words. Active voice only."
Better: "Write like Hemingway: short sentences, active voice, maximum 15 words. 
Example: 'We build tools. They save time. Your team wins.'"
```

**5. Organize by Use Case**
- Create separate brand voices for:
  - Main company brand
  - Sub-brands or divisions
  - Different product lines
  - Regional variations
  - B2B vs B2C audiences

### Writing Style Guidelines

Include in "Specific Instructions":
- **Grammar rules**: Oxford commas, contractions, capitalization
- **Formatting preferences**: Lists, headers, bold/italic usage
- **Voice characteristics**: First person vs third person
- **Cultural considerations**: Regional language variations
- **Industry standards**: Technical accuracy requirements

## Troubleshooting

### Cannot Create New Brand Voice

**Problem**: "New Company" button is disabled
**Solution**: 
- Check your brand voice limit in the usage display
- Delete unused brand voices
- Upgrade your plan for more slots

### Brand Voice Not Applying to Content

**Problem**: AI-generated content doesn't reflect brand guidelines
**Solution**:
- Verify brand voice is selected in the AI tool
- Ensure "Specific Instructions" are clearly written
- Check that company description is comprehensive
- Try refreshing the page and re-selecting brand voice

### Cannot Delete Brand Voice

**Problem**: Delete button not working
**Solution**:
- Check if you're in demo mode (feature disabled)
- Ensure you have proper permissions
- Contact support if issue persists

### Brand Voice Details Not Saving

**Problem**: Changes don't persist after saving
**Solution**:
- Check all required fields (*) are completed
- Verify internet connection
- Try saving again
- Clear browser cache
- Check browser console for errors

## Examples

### Example 1: Tech Startup Brand Voice

**Company Name**: TechFlow Solutions

**Description**: 
> TechFlow Solutions is a cutting-edge SaaS company that provides workflow automation tools for remote teams. Founded in 2020, we're passionate about eliminating busywork so teams can focus on meaningful collaboration. Our mission is to make remote work feel seamless and natural.

**Tone of Voice**: Casual

**Target Audience**: 
> Remote team managers and tech-savvy professionals aged 25-40 who value efficiency and modern work culture.

**Specific Instructions**:
```
- Use contractions (we're, you're) to sound conversational
- Avoid corporate jargon like "synergy" or "leverage"
- Keep sentences under 20 words
- Use second person (you/your) to address readers directly
- Include emojis sparingly (max 1-2 per paragraph)
- Write like you're explaining to a smart friend
```

### Example 2: Professional Services Brand Voice

**Company Name**: Sterling & Associates Legal

**Description**:
> Sterling & Associates is a full-service law firm specializing in corporate law, intellectual property, and contract negotiations. With over 30 years of combined experience, our attorneys provide sophisticated legal counsel while maintaining clear, accessible communication with clients. We believe legal services should be transparent, efficient, and client-focused.

**Tone of Voice**: Professional

**Target Audience**:
> Business executives, entrepreneurs, and corporate decision-makers requiring legal expertise for complex business matters.

**Specific Instructions**:
```
- Always use third person (Sterling & Associates, the firm)
- No contractions - write formally (we are, not we're)
- Define legal terms when first used
- Use Oxford commas
- Sentences between 15-25 words for clarity
- Avoid colloquialisms
- Maintain authoritative but approachable tone
- Include disclaimers when discussing legal matters
```
